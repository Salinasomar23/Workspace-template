The Thornwood · Western Border of Edrane

The First Night of the Long Rain

The bell rang from beneath the forest floor just as the rain began. Not above the trees, where bells belonged, and not from the village tower, which had fallen silent years before. It rang from somewhere deep under the roots, slow and heavy, as if the earth itself had remembered an old warning.

Liora stopped at the edge of the Thornwood with her lantern held low. The flame bent toward the trees, though there was no wind. Every branch ahead of her was wrapped in black thorns, and every thorn carried a drop of rain that shone like a watching eye. Behind her, the road home had already disappeared into mist.

Her grandmother had told her never to answer a bell in the forest. Bells called names. Bells opened doors. Bells woke things that had been buried for the safety of kings and children. But when the sound came again, Liora heard her own name inside it, clear as breath against her ear.
